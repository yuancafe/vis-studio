# Visual Identity Direction Frameworks

This reference distills reusable methodology from broader visual identity direction practice.

## Use It For

- converting strategy into visual direction
- building stronger concept territories
- improving moodboard rationale
- defining photography, typography, and color direction
- structuring logo and creative briefs

## Core Frameworks

### 1. Five-phase brand process

Use the larger arc:

1. research
2. strategy
3. design identity
4. create touchpoints
5. launch and governance

This package already maps onto that arc; use it to keep the work in the right phase.

### 2. Strategy-to-visual translation

Before visual exploration, define:

- 3-5 personality adjectives
- 2-3 word brand essence
- a single-minded proposition

Then translate them into:

- type behavior
- color posture
- composition logic
- imagery and lighting
- material and texture
- likely logo posture

### 3. Moodboard discipline

Good moodboards are not image dumps.

Every board should explain:

- what the direction is
- why it fits the strategy
- which signals are being explored
- what should not be copied

### 4. Logo brief structure

Capture:

- business context
- audience
- competitors
- style preferences
- references
- deliverables and uses
- what to avoid

### 5. Photography style framework

Define:

- lighting direction
- composition and framing
- color treatment
- people, styling, and wardrobe guidance

### 6. Typography and color reasoning

Do not only list fonts or hex codes. Explain why they fit the brand's role, audience, and perception target.
