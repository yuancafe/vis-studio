# Branching Discovery Questions

Ask 1-3 questions at a time. Move from diagnosis to narrowing.

## Intake gate

Before using the full question tree, quickly check whether you already have enough input for:

- business context
- target audience
- closest competitors or substitute brands
- current assets or reference materials
- phase-one deliverables

If the answer is no, ask the smallest missing set first.

Typical highest-leverage opening questions:

- Who are the closest competitors or reference brands we should benchmark against?
- What existing assets, guidelines, decks, packaging, pages, or social materials already exist?
- What exactly must be delivered in phase one?

If those are already provided, skip them and continue deeper into strategy.

## Stage 1. Route diagnosis

- Are we building from zero or evolving an existing identity?
- If assets exist, is the goal to preserve, refine, extend, or replace them?

Reason:
This determines the project trajectory and whether audit is central or only light-touch.

## Stage 2. Business context

- What does the business do now?
- What problem does it solve?
- What business change should the identity support?

Reason:
Identity is business infrastructure, not standalone styling.

## Stage 3. Audience

- Who buys now?
- Who must buy next?
- What are their anxieties, desires, and decision criteria?

Reason:
Brand meaning and visual tone must match a real audience.

## Stage 4. Competition

- Who are the closest competitors or substitutes?
- Which category codes are useful?
- What whitespace or differentiation can be owned?

Reason:
Without this, strategy becomes generic and visually derivative.

## Stage 5. Brand core

- Why does the brand exist beyond selling a product?
- What should people feel, believe, or expect from it?
- Which traits define the personality, and which traits must be avoided?

Reason:
This becomes the bridge into concept exploration.

## Stage 6. Current assets

If assets exist:

- What logo, colors, typography, website, templates, packaging, or guidelines exist?
- Which ones feel valuable?
- Which ones feel broken?

If no assets exist:

- What references or category examples feel relevant, and why?

Reason:
For existing brands, this reveals equity and debt. For new brands, it reveals initial taste and category expectations.

## Stage 7. Desired change

- What perception must change?
- Should this be evolutionary or more radical?
- What must stay recognizable, if anything?

Reason:
This sets the design ambition and risk level.

## Stage 8. Deliverables and constraints

- What must be delivered in phase one?
- What is explicitly phase two?
- What timeline, stakeholders, approvals, technical constraints, and accessibility needs matter?

Reason:
This prevents over-scoping and defines realistic output boundaries.

## Behavior note

If a user already supplied a detailed brief, competitor set, reference deck, and current assets, the skill should ask fewer questions. That is correct behavior. The goal is not to force a questionnaire; the goal is to close missing decision-critical gaps before strategy synthesis.
