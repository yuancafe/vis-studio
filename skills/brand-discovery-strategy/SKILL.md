# Brand Discovery Strategy

## Overview

Handle the full upstream phase of brand identity work in one skill. Keep discovery, asset audit, and strategy synthesis together so the resulting brief reflects business reality, competitive context, and current brand equity.

## Why This Skill Exists

NotebookLM synthesis of the `VIS` notebook indicates that discovery, audit, and strategy formulation should not be split across separate skills. Strategy emerges from discovery and audit; separating them creates positioning that ignores competitive reality or misreads existing assets.

## Use This Skill

- Start a new brand identity engagement
- Decide whether a project is from-zero, extension, refresh, or rebrand
- Audit an existing identity before deciding what to preserve or replace
- Build the strategic brief that later design phases must follow
- Surface likely application scope early so downstream production is not guessed later

## Workflow

### 1. Diagnose the route

First determine:

- from-zero brand
- existing logo but no real system
- fragmented legacy identity
- refresh / rebrand

### 2. Run integrated discovery

Ask in sequence, not all at once:

- business context
- audience
- competition
- current assets
- desired change
- scope
- likely applications and volume
- timeline, stakeholders, constraints

Load `references/branching-discovery-questions.md`.

### 3. Audit current assets when they exist

Assess:

- equity
- strategic fit
- technical quality
- scalability
- what should be preserved, refined, extended, or replaced

Load `references/integrated-discovery-audit.md`.

### 4. Form strategy from the evidence

Define:

- positioning
- audience priority
- differentiation
- purpose, vision, mission
- values
- personality
- verbal direction
- single-minded proposition
- preliminary application priorities

Translate strategy into visual-direction inputs before the next phase:

- 3-5 brand personality adjectives
- 2-3 word brand essence
- desired emotional response
- category codes to keep, reinterpret, or reject
- early photography and imagery posture

### 5. Produce the approved strategy brief

Create:

- strategic summary
- route recommendation
- approved problem statement
- design criteria for the next phase
- creative-brief core:
  - audience
  - SMP
  - tone
  - differentiation
  - visual guardrails
- initial scope posture:
  - strategy only
  - core identity
  - identity plus applications

### 6. Stop for approval

Do not move into concept exploration until the strategy brief is aligned.

Load `references/milestone-gates.md`.

## Outputs

- route type
- integrated audit summary
- positioning brief
- audience and competition synthesis
- preserve / refine / replace decisions
- approved design criteria
- strategy-to-visual translation inputs
- initial application-scope assumptions

## Non-Negotiables

- Never jump into moodboards before diagnosis
- Never separate audit findings from strategy synthesis
- Never treat founder taste as a substitute for positioning
- Never leave existing brand equity unexamined

## Hand Off

After approval, continue with `visual-concept-exploration`.
